Surviving The Collapse

By Luo Bing ♦ Jan 1 2015

A total collapse can be triggered may be triggered by various events, such as a financial crisis, an external trade shock, an adverse supply shock or the bursting of an economic bubble, and may be also brought about suddenly by way of a natural disaster or epidemic. A collapse is often accompanied by social chaos, civil unrest and sometimes a breakdown of law and order resulting an total anarchy. (examples)

Our fragile society is comprised of so many complex moving parts, which at any point could come crumbling down around our ears. Very few people are ready for such an occurance, and it’s both frightening and necessary to understand that at any given moment we are only three square meals away from total anarchy.

The skills that are deemed to be important in our modern world will cease to be relevant, and a much more practical set of survival skills and preparedness will be required. It is therefore prudent to be prepared and educate yourself in the event that such disasters may come to pass.
